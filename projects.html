Personal Project

Bunny Carrot Maze Based on Q-Learning

Mar 2026

Problem

Built a reinforcement-learning maze game where a bunny must collect a carrot before reaching the exit while avoiding traps and managing HP. The main challenge was delayed reward learning in mazes with different levels of long-horizon difficulty.

Data

The environment uses procedurally generated grid mazes with randomized walls, carrot, exit, poison traps, and medkits. I evaluated the agent on three generated maps using win rate, reward, training logs, and step count.

Approach

Implemented a Python and Pygame environment plus a tabular Q-learning agent with epsilon-greedy exploration, Q-table updates, and state encoding for position, key status, HP, target direction, nearby walls, and nearby traps.

Outcome / Impact

The agent reached 100% final win rate on Map 1 and Map 2 and reduced solution steps from more than 199 to about 23-24. Map 3 exposed the limitation of tabular Q-learning on longer and more complex maze topology.

My Contribution

Sole author and developer. I designed the game mechanics, UI, maze generation, reward logic, Q-learning agent, experiments, and analysis of success and failure cases.

Personal Project

Deep Learning Experiments on Skin Cancer Classification

Mar 2026

Problem

Investigated how to improve nine-class skin lesion classification under small, noisy, and highly imbalanced medical image data. The project tested whether data cleaning, minority-class augmentation, and channel attention could improve performance and stability.

Data

Used the ISIC Skin Cancer dataset from Kaggle, with 2,357 dermatoscopic images across nine diagnostic categories. The original split included 2,239 training images and 118 test images, followed by duplicate inspection, resizing to 200 x 200, grayscale conversion, and normalization.

Approach

Compared original data, all-class augmentation, and minority-class targeted augmentation using a four-layer CNN baseline. Then trained ResNet50 and SE-ResNet50 with Adam, categorical cross-entropy, early stopping, and image transformations.

Outcome / Impact

Minority-class targeted augmentation achieved the best CNN baseline accuracy at 0.36780. SE-ResNet50 slightly outperformed ResNet50 in accuracy and AUC while showing more stable convergence.

My Contribution

Sole author. I completed dataset inspection and cleaning, preprocessing, augmentation design, CNN and ResNet experiments, metric comparison, and written analysis.

EVA_Momenta: Physics-Grounded Visual Language Navigation

Apr 2026

Problem

Vision-language navigation systems can choose semantically plausible targets but still fail local physical judgment, such as unsafe moves, occluded geometry, or uncertain boundaries.

Data

Used a structured scene-generation pipeline built from room specifications, object assets, layout constraints, and navigation supervision. The system renders RGB, depth, stereo observations, and simulation traces across indoor scenes using MuJoCo and Isaac Sim style backends.

Approach

Adapted a Qwen3-VL 2B instruction model into a compact navigation controller with FORWARD, LEFT, and RIGHT actions using QLoRA fine-tuning. Added FFSP perception-to-physics components, including stereo reconstruction, SAM tracking, 6D state estimation, Newton-style rollout, and collision-aware filtering.

Outcome / Impact

Fine-tuning improved success rates from 0.05 to 0.65 on real-scan and from 0.13 to 0.82 in simulation. The FFSP-enhanced version further reached 0.83 and 0.87, showing the value of explicit physical judgment.

My Contribution

Responsible for the Backbone Training part: compact Qwen3-VL navigation interface, training-corpus design, QLoRA adaptation, fine-tuning validation, and analysis of semantic controller gains before FFSP.

Group Project

Controllability Versus Robustness in Flower Recognition

Mar 2026

Problem

Studied whether a controllable white-box flower classifier can remain competitive with black-box neural networks while staying robust under adversarial perturbations. The focus was the tradeoff between auditability and robustness.

Data

Used the Kaggle/TensorFlow flower benchmark with five classes. The official split included 3,540 training images, 80 validation images, and 50 balanced test images; after duplicate removal, 3,616 development images were used.

Approach

Built explicit white-box representations using HOG, HSV color histograms, LBP texture, global statistics, masks, crops, and semantic flower-aware features. Compared Logistic Regression, SVMs, Random Forest, VGG16, and ResNet18 under a shared Square Attack.

Outcome / Impact

The semantic Random Forest reached 0.680 clean accuracy, matching VGG16 and approaching ResNet18 at 0.700. Under attack, it dropped to 0.320 while VGG16 and ResNet18 stayed at 0.640 and 0.660.

My Contribution

Responsible for research and experiments on neural network methods, including the VGG16 and ResNet18 comparison side used to benchmark the white-box pipeline.

Group Project

Center-Aware Dataset Distillation for MNIST-Family Classification

Apr 2026

Problem

Studied how to compress multiple visually related but semantically mismatched MNIST-family domains into a compact synthetic training set. The challenge was preserving cross-domain structure under a shared 10-class index space.

Data

Used MNIST, FashionMNIST, EMNISTDigits, USPS, and KMNIST. Inputs were converted to grayscale and resized to 28 x 28 when needed, then evaluated using per-domain, mean, minimum, and maximum accuracy.

Approach

Proposed center-aware dataset distillation using a frozen teacher feature space to compute global class centers and domain-to-center discrepancies. Synthetic budget was allocated by domain displacement and optimized with class, feature, reconstruction, and smoothness losses.

Outcome / Impact

At a fixed 10k synthetic budget, center-aware allocation improved mean accuracy from 0.5508 to 0.6044 and minimum-domain accuracy from 0.2740 to 0.3744. Adding 50k synthetic samples to MoE improved mean accuracy from 0.9409 to 0.9454.

My Contribution

Responsible for inter-dataset discrepancy and s-ODD-related analysis, including distance-structure study across the five datasets and its connection to the center-aware method motivation.
